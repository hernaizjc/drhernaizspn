Frequently Asked Questions

Information about endoscopic sinus surgery (ESS): what to expect before, during, and after the procedure.

ESS is a procedure designed to widen the natural drainage openings of the paranasal sinuses. It is performed under general anesthesia using an endoscope introduced through the nostril — no external scars, no change in the shape of the nose.

The main goals of ESS are: (i) improve natural sinus drainage; (ii) remove inflamed tissue; and (iii) optimize the nasal cavity for topical treatments (e.g., medicated nasal rinses).

ESS does not necessarily "cure" chronic sinusitis, but it allows medical treatments to work more effectively. Some patients — particularly those with nasal polyps — will require periodic office visits to control inflammation and prevent recurrence.
Every surgical procedure carries potential risks. The most common complication of ESS is postoperative bleeding, which is usually minimal. Major complications are rare (approximately 1 in 10,000 cases).

The main complications to be aware of are injury to the eye or orbit and injury to the skull base — both are detected and addressed during surgery. Injury to neurovascular structures (e.g., carotid artery, cranial nerves) is extremely rare.
Depending on your case, I may prescribe medications (antibiotics, anti-inflammatories, etc.) one week before surgery — not all patients need this. If you take blood thinners (aspirin, clopidogrel, warfarin), inform me beforehand as they may need to be stopped. Also stop vitamins and supplements (ginger, ginkgo biloba, omega-3, etc.) due to mild anticoagulant effects. Medications for chronic conditions (diabetes, hypertension) do not need to be stopped.

On the day of surgery, apply two sprays of Afrin® nasal spray into each nostril when you wake up. At the hospital, repeat two sprays every 30 minutes until taken to the OR. Arrive at least one hour early, always accompanied by a responsible adult.
ESS is outpatient surgery — you go home the same day. It causes minimal pain and is well tolerated. Some bloody mucus drainage is normal in the first few days; for mild bleeding, apply Afrin nasal spray.

Common symptoms in the first week include: nasal congestion, reduced smell, facial pressure, low-grade fever (37.0–37.8°C / 98.6–100°F), and fatigue. These are due to dissolvable sponges placed in the sinuses that are removed at your first postoperative visit. These sponges are NOT nasal packs — you will be able to breathe normally through your nose.

⚠ WARNING SIGNS: If you experience heavy bleeding, fever above 38.5°C (101.3°F), a severe headache (worst of your life), swelling around the eyes, or double vision — go to the emergency room immediately and contact Dr. Hernaiz urgently.
Avoid intense exercise and blowing your nose for the first 7–10 days. Do not insert objects into your nose or pull out any sutures. Resume your normal diet but avoid spicy or very fatty foods the first week.

Perform nasal rinses with the Sinus Rinse® system using saline and budesonide 0.5 mg/ml: three times daily the first week, twice daily the week after sponge removal, then once daily until further notice. Post-ESS infections are extremely rare — no postoperative antibiotics are needed.
I will see you one week after surgery to remove the sponges and clear any clots from your sinuses. This visit can be slightly uncomfortable, so I recommend taking 1 tablet of Tramacet® 30 minutes beforehand.

Further follow-up visits depend on your recovery and are generally comfortable — no premedication required.
